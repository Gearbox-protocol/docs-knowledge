# Pool

The Gearbox Pool is a passive, ERC-4626 compliant vault that holds a single underlying asset (e.g., USDC, WETH), issues yield-bearing tokens to depositors, distributes capital to lending strategies, and isolates risk through debt ceilings.

This page covers the deposit/withdrawal interface, the yield accrual mechanism, the capital distribution model, and the risk isolation guarantees — information required to evaluate the Pool as a liquidity venue or integrate with its ERC-4626 interface.

---

## How Deposits and Withdrawals Work

The Pool holds one underlying asset and issues **Diesel Tokens** (dTokens) to depositors as a receipt of liquidity provision.

**ERC-4626 compliance.** The Pool implements the full ERC-4626 (Tokenized Vault Standard) interface: `deposit()`, `withdraw()`, `redeem()`, and all associated view functions. Any ERC-4626-aware integration — aggregator, portfolio tracker, vault composer — works with the Gearbox Pool without custom code.

**Diesel Token properties:**

- Fungible and transferable. Diesel Tokens can be used as collateral in other DeFi protocols, transferred between wallets, or held in multisigs.
- Non-rebasing. Wallet balances do not change over time. Yield accrues through the exchange rate (see below).

**Liquidity.** Deposits and withdrawals are instant, subject to available unborrowed liquidity. If the entire Pool balance is lent out to Credit Suites, withdrawals queue until capital is returned.

**Inflation protection.** The Pool mints 1e5 "dead shares" on deployment to prevent vault inflation attacks — a known ERC-4626 vulnerability where early depositors can manipulate the share price.

## How Yield Accrues (Non-Rebasing Tokens)

Diesel Tokens differ from rebasing tokens (e.g., Aave's aTokens) in a way that matters for integrators and accountants.

**Constant balance, increasing value.** The Diesel Token wallet balance does not change. Yield accrues through the exchange rate between dTokens and the underlying asset:

> **Exchange Rate = Total Assets (Principal + Interest) / Total Supply of dTokens**

As borrowers pay interest, the Pool's total underlying assets grow while the dToken supply remains constant. The exchange rate increases. A depositor who received 1,000 dUSDC at an exchange rate of 1.00 can later redeem them at 1.05, receiving 1,050 USDC.

**Yield sources.** Borrow rates are determined by two components:

1. **Base interest rate** — a utilization-driven rate calculated by the Interest Rate Model. Higher utilization → higher rates.
2. **Quota rates** — collateral-specific premiums applied to assets with exposure limits. These are set per token and added on top of the base rate.

**Integrator note.** The non-rebasing design has implications for tax treatment, accounting systems, and downstream protocol logic. Integrators should read the token's value via `convertToAssets()` rather than tracking balance changes.

## How Capital Flows to Lending Strategies

The Pool has no knowledge of individual borrowers, collateral types, or liquidation logic. It performs one function: lending capital to approved Credit Suites up to defined limits.

Each **Credit Suite** borrows from the Pool to fund Credit Accounts. The Credit Suite enforces its own risk parameters — Liquidation Thresholds, allowed collateral assets, borrowing limits, adapter permissions. All risk complexity lives in the Credit Suite layer; the Pool remains a passive, auditable capital reservoir.

This separation of concerns keeps the Pool lightweight:

- The Pool tracks aggregate utilization, not per-borrower positions.
- The Pool enforces debt ceilings, not collateral quality.
- The Pool computes supply rates, not liquidation conditions.

---

## How Risk Is Isolated Across Strategies

A single Pool can fund multiple Credit Suites simultaneously. Risk isolation is enforced through **Debt Ceilings** — the maximum capital each Credit Suite can borrow.

The Market Curator sets these ceilings based on the risk profile of each strategy. If a Credit Suite suffers bad debt — a collateral collapse, an oracle failure, a liquidation shortfall — the loss is contained within that Suite's allocation. The Pool's exposure is bounded by the ceiling, and remaining liquidity is protected.

This is the mechanism behind "One Pool, Many Markets": diversified utilization without contagion. A lender depositing into the Pool benefits from yield generated by all connected strategies, while the worst-case loss from any single strategy is capped at its Debt Ceiling.

---

## How Lenders Are Protected (First-Loss Capital)

Gearbox V3 implements an automated **First-Loss Capital** buffer. Protocol revenue is retained within the system until specific safety targets are met, rather than being distributed immediately.

This mechanism prioritizes protocol solvency over profit distribution. The insurance buffer absorbs losses before they reach passive lenders — an automated first line of defense against bad debt events in any connected Credit Suite.
